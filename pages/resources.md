Unfortunately the process isn't quite as straight forward as one would hope, particularly for an older version of AVR Studio. The guide also gives my reasons why you might want to run an older version of this software.

## MIDI files on the net

* [www.classicalarchives.com](http://www.classicalarchives.com)  
An excellent source of classical MIDI files available both free and by subscription
* [www.mutopiaproject.org](http://www.mutopiaproject.org)  
A large selection of out of copyright music available as MIDI
* [www.partnersinrhyme.com](http://www.partnersinrhyme.com/midi)  
Lots of rock, jazz, dance, movie and broadway MIDI files
* [www.aitech.ac.jp/~ckelly](http://www.aitech.ac.jp/~ckelly/SMF.html)  
Composers offering MIDI files on the net
* Or just [Google](http://www.google.com) for your favorite!  
Don't forget to add MIDI to your search.
